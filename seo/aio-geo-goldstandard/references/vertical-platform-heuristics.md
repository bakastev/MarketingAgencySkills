# Vertical and platform heuristics (GEO / AIO)

Use when the brief names an **industry**, **target AI surface**, or **branded subject entity**. These are **planning heuristics**, not universal laws—verify quantitative claims against **primary sources** before citing numbers in client work.

## Vertical shapes the “right” shape of the page

- **Citation-friendly patterns differ by industry.** Longer copy with more lists may help in some B2B/SaaS contexts and hurt in others (e.g. sensitive health topics often favor **fewer** H2s and a very direct lead).
- **Choose** heading depth and list density **after** vertical + intent—not “max headings by default.”

## Owned media is rarely the whole game

- Many GEO reports stress **community and third-party** sources (forums, video, reviews) alongside the brand site. A strong owned page is still necessary; it may not be sufficient for **entity consensus** across models.
- Discuss ethical presence where consensus forms (accurate listings, legitimate comparisons, expert commentary)—not spam.

## Entity consensus and “ghost citations”

- A URL in a source list without the **brand or entity name in the answer text** often yields weak brand gain (“ghost citation”).
- **Mitigation:** In the visible copy, **grammatically bind** the canonical brand/organization/person name to the main facts—not only a bare URL or footnote.

## Platform fragmentation

- ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI surfaces can **diverge strongly** in what they cite. Optimize for **stated** target surfaces; do not assume success transfers one-to-one.

## Recency

- Models often favor **recent** documents. Use **honest** `dateModified` / visible updates and periodic content reviews on **money / YMYL / core entity** pages. Do not fake freshness.

## Traffic and conversion nuance

- AI summaries can reduce clicks to classic blue links (“zero-click” narratives vary by study design). Cited traffic can show **higher intent**—landing pages still need **clear next steps** and **trust**.

## Near-universal content signals (still contextual)

- **Positive:** visible date + concrete numbers, deadlines, or metrics near the answer; **declarative** opening; **all-in or flat** structure (see main `SKILL.md`).
- **Negative:** **price-led** opening when intent is not pricing/finance; **hedge-led** opening.

## Specificity over “knowledge-graph fame”

- A well-known entity string does not replace **dense, niche-specific** facts. Prioritize information gain.

## Chunks and “answer capsules”

- Retrieval often uses **small semantic units**. After each material **H2**, place a **compact answer block** (planning guide ~50 words—tune by vertical).
- Rough planning aid: aim for **one clearly supported main fact per ~80 words** of explanatory prose where appropriate—**never invent statistics**.
